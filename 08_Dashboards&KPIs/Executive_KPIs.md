# Executive KPIs & Dashboards  
## AI-Powered Revenue Cycle Management (RCM) Product

---

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the **executive-level KPIs and dashboards** used to measure the performance, impact, and business value of the AI-Powered RCM Product.

The KPIs provide leadership with **clear, actionable visibility** into revenue performance, operational efficiency, and risk areas without requiring deep operational or technical involvement.

---

## 2. Executive Dashboard Objectives

The executive dashboards are designed to:
- Provide a single source of truth for revenue performance
- Highlight risks and opportunities early
- Support data-driven decision-making
- Track the impact of product enhancements over time

---

## 3. Core Executive KPIs

### 3.1 Financial Performance KPIs

#### Net Collection Rate
- Measures the percentage of collectible revenue successfully collected  
- Indicates overall revenue effectiveness  

#### Accounts Receivable (A/R) Days
- Tracks the average number of days claims remain unpaid  
- Lower A/R days indicate faster cash realization  

#### Gross vs Net Revenue Leakage
- Identifies revenue lost due to denials, write-offs, or underpayments  

---

### 3.2 Denial & Quality KPIs

#### Claim Denial Rate
- Percentage of claims denied by payers  
- Tracked by payer, provider, and denial category  

#### First Pass Yield (FPY)
- Percentage of claims paid on first submission  
- Strong indicator of front-end and mid-cycle quality  

---

### 3.3 Operational Efficiency KPIs

#### Productivity per FTE
- Claims processed or resolved per full-time equivalent  
- Measures operational efficiency gains  

#### AR Follow-Up Effectiveness
- Percentage of AR resolved within defined SLA windows  

---

### 3.4 Risk & Compliance KPIs

#### SLA Compliance Rate
- Measures adherence to internal and external SLAs  

#### Exception Rate
- Percentage of claims requiring manual intervention  

---

## 4. AI Impact Metrics

### 4.1 Denial Prediction Accuracy
- Accuracy of AI-generated denial risk predictions  
- Monitored to maintain trust and effectiveness  

### 4.2 Prevented Denials
- Estimated number of denials avoided due to proactive intervention  

### 4.3 AI Adoption Rate
- Percentage of users actively using AI-assisted workflows  

---

## 5. Executive Dashboard Views

### 5.1 Revenue Overview
- Net collections trend  
- A/R aging distribution  
- Revenue leakage summary  

---

### 5.2 Denial Analytics
- Denial trends over time  
- Top denial reasons  
- Payer-level denial performance  

---

### 5.3 Operational Health
- Workload distribution  
- SLA adherence  
- Bottleneck identification  

---

## 6. Data Governance & Privacy Considerations

- Dashboards operate on **aggregated and de-identified data**  
- No display or exposure of PHI or PII  
- Access restricted to executive and authorized roles  

---

## 7. Review Cadence & Ownership

- Dashboards reviewed weekly by operations leadership  
- Executive summaries reviewed monthly  
- KPI definitions owned by Product Management  
- Continuous refinement based on business needs  

---

## 8. Decision Support & Actions

Each dashboard is designed to support **specific executive actions**, including:
- Resource reallocation  
- Policy and workflow adjustments  
- Investment prioritization  
- Risk mitigation  

---

## 9. Success Indicators

The dashboards are considered effective when:
- Executives can identify issues without operational deep-dives  
- KPI trends clearly reflect product changes  
- Decisions are made faster and with higher confidence  

---

## 10. Summary

Executive KPIs and dashboards provide leadership with a **clear, concise view of revenue performance and operational health**, ensuring that product investments translate into measurable business outcomes.

---

*This KPI framework evolves as product capabilities and business priorities mature.*
